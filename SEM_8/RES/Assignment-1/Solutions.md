### 1. Classify the energy resources based on commercial application with examples. 

* **Commercial Energy Resources:** These are traded in the market for a specific price and form the backbone of modern industrial economies. Examples include coal, petroleum, natural gas, and electricity.
* **Non-Commercial Energy Resources:** These are typically gathered by the user and are not generally bought or sold in mainstream, formal markets. Examples include firewood, animal dung, and agricultural waste.

### 2. What do you mean by green power? Give some examples. 
* Ans: Green power refers to electricity generated from environmentally friendly, sustainable sources that produce zero or negligible greenhouse gas emissions. Examples include solar power, wind power, geothermal energy, biogas, and small, low-impact hydroelectric systems.

### 3. Explain the greenhouse effect. Mention some of the greenhouse gasses. 
* Ans: The greenhouse effect is the physical process where incoming solar radiation passes through the Earth's atmosphere, but the resulting outward heat (infrared radiation) is absorbed and trapped by certain gases, warming the planet. The primary greenhouse gases are Carbon Dioxide (CO2), Methane (CH4), Nitrous Oxide (N2O), Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), and Water Vapor.

### 4. Write the consequences of global warming. 
* Ans: The measurable consequences include rising global sea levels due to thermal expansion and melting ice caps, an increased frequency of extreme weather events (such as intense hurricanes and prolonged droughts), massive disruption of ecosystems leading to biodiversity loss, and severe threats to global agricultural yields and food security.

### 5. What are the different types of solar energy. 

* **Solar Photovoltaic (PV):** Technology that converts sunlight directly into electrical current using semiconductor materials.
* **Solar Thermal:** Technology that captures heat from the sun to warm water/air directly or to boil water into steam to drive electricity-generating turbines.

### 6. What do you understand by Passive and Active solar energy? What is the difference between them? 

* **Passive Solar Energy:** Uses the structural design of a building (like south-facing windows and thermal mass materials) to naturally collect, store, and distribute solar heat without mechanical assistance.
* **Active Solar Energy:** Utilizes mechanical or electrical equipment, such as pumps, fans, or photovoltaic panels, to capture, convert, and distribute the sun's energy.
* **Difference:** Active systems require external energy inputs and hardware to function, whereas passive systems rely entirely on thermodynamics and architectural design.

### 7. What do you understand by beam radiation? What is the desirable value with respect to global radiation? 
* Ans: Beam (or direct) radiation is the solar radiation that travels in a straight line from the sun to the Earth's surface without being scattered or absorbed by the atmosphere. For technologies that concentrate solar power, you want beam radiation to make up the vast majority of the global radiation (typically 80% or higher during clear skies) to ensure high operational efficiency.

### 8. How water vapor controls the beam radiation. 
* Ans: Water vapor is a highly effective atmospheric absorber. It absorbs specific wavelengths of incoming solar energy (heavily in the infrared spectrum) and scatters the light. High humidity or dense cloud cover aggressively attenuates beam radiation, converting much of it into weaker, diffuse radiation before it hits the ground.

### 9. Explain about solar constant and inclination angle. 

* **Solar Constant:** The rate at which solar electromagnetic radiation strikes a unit area perpendicular to the sun's rays at the outer edge of Earth's atmosphere. The accepted hard value is approximately 1361 W/m².
* **Inclination Angle (Solar Altitude):** The angle between the sun's direct rays and the horizontal plane of the Earth's surface at a given location. This angle strictly dictates the intensity of the solar radiation received; closer to 90 degrees means maximum intensity, while lower angles spread the energy over a larger area, reducing intensity.

### 10. What are the different renewable energy policies by 2025. 
Standard global and national policies driving 2025 targets focus on rapid grid integration and scaling capacity. Core mechanisms include:

* **Renewable Purchase Obligations (RPOs):** Mandates requiring energy distributors to source a fixed percentage of their electricity from renewables.
* **Feed-in Tariffs:** Guaranteed, above-market price contracts for renewable energy producers to ensure investment security.
* **Capital Subsidies:** Direct financial incentives for residential and commercial solar installations.
* **Green Energy Corridors:** Massive infrastructure investments to upgrade grid transmission lines to handle the variable power generation inherent to wind and solar.
