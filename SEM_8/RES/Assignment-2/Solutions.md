### Q1: What are the definitions of a PV cell, module, and array?

* **PV Cell:** The fundamental semiconductor device that converts sunlight directly into direct current (DC) electricity via the photoelectric effect.
* **PV Module:** A packaged, weather-proof assembly of interconnected PV cells (commonly referred to as a solar panel).
* **PV Array:** A complete power-generating unit consisting of multiple PV modules wired in series and/or parallel to output a required voltage and current.

### Q2: Explain the photovoltaic principle and define a PV system.

* **Photovoltaic Principle:** When photons strike a semiconductor, their energy excites electrons, breaking them free from atomic bonds. The internal electric field of a p-n junction forces these free electrons to flow in a specific direction, generating a DC current.
* **PV System:** A complete functional setup that includes the PV array to generate DC power, a charge controller to regulate voltage, a battery bank to store energy, and an inverter to convert DC to alternating current (AC) for standard electrical loads.

### Q3: What is the solar constant and what is its value in MJ/m²/h?

* **Definition:** The rate at which solar energy hits a surface perpendicular to the sun's rays at the outer edge of the Earth's atmosphere, assuming the Earth is at its mean distance from the sun.
* **Standard Value:** 1367 W/m².
* **Conversion:** * 1367 W = 1367 Joules/second.
* Multiply by 3600 seconds: 1367 × 3600 = 4,921,200 J/m²/h.
* Convert to Megajoules: 4.92 MJ/m²/h.

### Q4: Define Air Mass (AM) and state the condition for AM = 2.

* **Definition:** Air Mass is the relative optical path length that solar radiation travels through the Earth's atmosphere compared to the path length when the sun is directly overhead (the zenith).
* **Formula:** $AM = \frac{1}{\cos(\theta_z)}$
* **Condition for AM = 2:** This occurs when the secant of the zenith angle ($\theta_z$) is 2. Therefore, $\cos(\theta_z)$ must equal 0.5, meaning the zenith angle is exactly 60°.

### Q5: Name the instrument used to measure global radiation and explain its construction and working principle.

* **Instrument:** Pyranometer.
* **Construction:** It features a black-coated thermopile sensor housed under two concentric hemispherical glass domes. The domes protect the sensor from wind and thermal radiation but allow shortwave solar radiation to pass.
* **Working Principle:** The black surface absorbs direct and diffuse solar radiation, heating up. The temperature difference between this surface and the instrument's casing generates a small voltage directly proportional to the incident radiation.

### Q6: What is the basic principle of Solar PV energy conversion?

It relies on the generation of electron-hole pairs within a semiconductor when it absorbs incident light. The built-in potential barrier (the p-n junction) separates these charge carriers—sweeping electrons to the n-type side and holes to the p-type side. This separation creates an electromotive force (EMF), which drives an electric current when an external load is connected.

### Q7: What is the Energy Payback Period?

The Energy Payback Period (EPBP) is the total amount of time a renewable energy system must operate to generate the exact amount of energy that was consumed during its entire lifecycle. This includes energy used for its manufacturing, installation, maintenance, and eventual disposal.

### Q8: Differentiate between Stand-Alone and Grid-Interactive SPV systems.

* **Stand-Alone System:** Operates entirely independently of the utility grid. It uses batteries to store excess energy for use during the night or on cloudy days. (Flow: PV Array → Charge Controller → Battery Bank → Inverter → AC Load).
* **Grid-Interactive (Grid-Tied) System:** Operates in tandem with the utility grid and typically lacks batteries. Excess generated power is injected into the grid, and power is drawn from the grid when the local load exceeds generation. (Flow: PV Array → Grid-Tied Inverter → AC Load / Utility Grid).

### Q9: How are solar cells classified based on their active material?

* **Crystalline Silicon (c-Si):** Includes Monocrystalline (cut from a single continuous crystal; highest efficiency) and Polycrystalline (melted silicon fragments; lower cost and efficiency).
* **Thin-Film:** Layers of semiconductor material only a few micrometers thick, including Amorphous Silicon (a-Si), Cadmium Telluride (CdTe), and Copper Indium Gallium Selenide (CIGS).
* **Emerging/Third Generation:** Includes Perovskite, organic photovoltaics (OPV), and quantum dot cells.

### Q10: Describe the operation of a Central Tower Receiver Power Plant.

A vast field of computer-controlled, dual-axis tracking mirrors (heliostats) reflects and concentrates direct sunlight onto a single receiver at the top of a central tower. A heat transfer fluid (like molten salt) circulates inside the receiver, absorbing the concentrated thermal energy. This fluid is then used to produce high-pressure steam, driving a conventional turbine-generator to produce electricity.
