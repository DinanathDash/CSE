### **1. Discuss the etymology of the word 'Veda'. How this relates to Sound?**
**Ans:** From the Sanskrit root vid ("to know"). It links to sound because Vedas are Shruti (heard); they rely on precise phonetic vibration (Nada, Swara) to preserve meaning and structure reality, not written text.

### **2. List the four processes recognized in production of speech, as per the paper by 'Honda'.**
**Ans:** 
1. Respiration (lungs/airflow)
2. Phonation (vocal fold vibration)
3. Resonance (vocal tract filtering)
4. Articulation (tongue/lip movement)

### **3. What are the two keywords in the Vedic literature to describe sound field. Why do they lack English equivalents?**
**Ans:** *Nada* (primordial vibration) and *Akasha* (spatial ether). English lacks equivalents because Western terminology treats sound strictly as mechanical waves, while Vedic concepts fuse physical acoustics with universal consciousness.

### **4. Explain the 4 stages of speech sounds, as per Veda.**
**Ans:** 
1. **Para:** Transcendental, unmanifest root.
2. **Pashyanti:** Visualized, cognitive impulse.
3. **Madhyama:** Mental, structured internal thought.
4. **Vaikhari:** Gross, audible physical speech.

### **5. Explain the creation of universe as per PPMV model and introspection for understanding life through VMPP model.**
**Ans:** 
* **PPMV (Creation):** Macrocosmic involution. Pure consciousness (Para) condenses outward into physical reality (Vaikhari).
* **VMPP (Introspection):** Microcosmic evolution. A practitioner traces gross physical sound (Vaikhari) inward to reach absolute silence (Para).

### **7. Explain about 3 industries that flourished during the medieval era, based on chemistry.**
**Ans:** 
1. **Textile Dyeing:** Use of chemical mordants (alum) to fix natural dyes.
2. **Metallurgy:** High-temperature zinc distillation and Wootz steel crucible processing.
3. **Perfumery:** Distillation of essential oils (using *deg* and *bhapka* apparatuses).

### **8. How Arabians influenced India?**
**Ans:** Introduced Unani medicine, advanced paper-making (replacing palm leaves), architectural true arches and domes, and catalyzed the formation of the Urdu language.

### **9. Explain Any 4 works done during the period of Emperor Akbar because of his interest.**
**Ans:** 
1. **Maktab Khana:** Bureau for translating Sanskrit texts to Persian.
2. **Ibadat Khana:** Hall for interfaith theological debate.
3. **Din-i-Ilahi:** Syncretic spiritual and ethical code.
4. **Navaratnas:** Patronage of nine specialized cultural and administrative experts.

### **10. Explain the reforms in Agriculture, that suited centralized administration during Mughal period.**
**Ans:** 
1. Standardized measurement using the *Bigha* (bamboo poles joined by iron rings).
2. Strict land classification based on actual soil fertility.
3. The Dahsala System (revenue fixed on a 10-year rolling average).
4. Direct state-to-peasant contracts (*Patta* and *Qabuliyat*) to bypass middlemen.
